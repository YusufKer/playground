Summary of Lottery Operator Rules

Selection of Numbers

To play French Loto, a player selects five numbers from a pool of 1 to 49 and one Chance Number from a pool of 1 to 10. Players may select their own numbers or use the ‘quick pick’ function to have the numbers randomly selected.

Prizes and Odds

There are ten different ways for players to win a prize.

*The percentage shown for prize tiers 2 through to 8, is the percentage of the ‘Estimated Prize Fund’ after the value of Prize Tiers 1, 9 and 10 have been deducted.

The underlying lottery is conducted on a pari-mutuel lottery basis, how much you win depends on the number of winning tickets in that same tier as well as the number of tickets sold in the particular underlying lottery draw. With the exception of the ‘1 Number + Chance Number’ and ‘Chance Number’ prize categories (Prize Tiers 9 and 10) which are fixed prizes.

Only one prize per game can be won in each draw, if a game qualifies as a winner across more than one prize category than the highest prize will be paid to the winning player.

Draw Times

France Loto draws are conducted three times each week on Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays at 8:20p.m. (Central European Time). The draw time converts to Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays, Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST).

Jackpot Rollover

There is a jackpot cap on French Loto, the jackpot can roll over 34 times. If the jackpot is not won when it reaches the jackpot cap the jackpot prize amount is ‘rolled down’ and shared between the next highest winning prize category.

Jackpot Payout

Jackpot winners receive their prize as a lump-sum payment.

Tax

Lottery winnings are not currently taxed in France, where the matching tickets are purchased in the underlying lottery.

Promotional Prizes

We are unable to offer any local promotional prizes that are offered by the underlying lottery operators from time to time. The ten different ways for players to win a prize as listed above are the valid prizes we offer.
