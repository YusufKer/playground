Summary of Lottery Operatore Rules

Selection of Numbers

To play SuperEnalotto, a player selects six numbers from a pool of 1 to 90. Players may select their own numbers or use the ‘quick pick’ function to have the numbers randomly selected.

Prizes and Odds

There are seven different ways for players to win a prize.

Prize Tier Match % of Prize Fund Odds
1 6 Numbers 17.4% 1 in 622,614,630
2 5 Numbers + Jolly Number 13% 1 in 103,769,105
3 5 Numbers 4.2% 1 in 1,250,230
4 4 Numbers 4.2% 1 in 11,907
5 3 Numbers 12.8% 1 in 327
6 2 Numbers 40% 1 in 21.51
7 Instant Win Prize 8.4% 1 in 500

The overall odds of winning a prize are 1 in 20.

The ‘Jolly Number’ gives an additional chance to players who have matched five numbers, if they also match the ‘Jolly Number’ then they will move from the third division prize (5 numbers) to the second division prize (5 numbers plus Jolly Number). A player does not select a ‘Jolly Number’ when purchasing a ticket, this is an additional number drawn from the remaining 84 numbers left in the draw.

The underlying lottery is conducted on a pari-mutuel lottery basis, how much you win depends on the number of winning tickets in that same tier as well as the number of tickets sold in the particular underlying lottery draw.

Only one prize per game can be won in each draw, if a game qualifies as a winner across more than one prize category than the highest prize will be paid to the winning player.

Instant Win Prize

Instant win is a supplementary game which gives players another chance to win a prize. A ‘Magic Square’ is printed on each ticket containing four numbers and if all four numbers are present in your ticket you may win €25.

Draw Times

SuperEnalotto draws are conducted three times each week, on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays at 8:00p.m. (Central European Time). The draw time converts to Wednesdays, Fridays and Sundays, Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST).

Jackpot Rollover

There is no jackpot cap on SuperEnalotto, the jackpot continues to grow until it is won.

Jackpot Payout

Jackpot winners receive their prize as a lump-sum payment.

Tax

The Italian government apply a tax of 20% on lottery winnings greater than €500. This tax is deducted from the prize amount prior to the payout from the underlying lottery operator.

Promotional Prizes

We are unable to offer any local promotional prizes that are offered by the underlying lottery operators from time to time. The seven different ways for players to win a prize as listed above are the valid prizes we offer.
