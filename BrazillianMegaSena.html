Summary of Lottery Operator Rules

Selection of Numbers

To play Brazilian Mega-Sena, a player selects six numbers from a pool of 1 to 60. Players may select their own numbers or use the ‘quick pick’ function to have the numbers randomly selected.

Prizes and Odds

There are three different ways for players to win a prize.

Prize Tier Match Estimated Prize Fund Odds
1 6 Numbers 35% 1 in 50,063,860
2 5 Numbers 19% 1 in 154,518
3 4 Numbers 19% 1 in 2,332

The overall odds of winning a prize are 1 in 2,298.

It is noted that 22% of the ‘Prize Fund’ is kept aside for a special game that is held every five draws and 5% of the ‘Prize Fund’ is set aside for the ‘Mega da Virada’ (New Year’s Eve) draw.

The underlying lottery is conducted on a pari-mutuel lottery basis, how much you win depends on the number of winning tickets in that same tier as well as the number of tickets sold in the particular underlying lottery draw.

Only one prize per game can be won in each draw, if a game qualifies as a winner across more than one prize category than the highest prize will be paid to the winning player.

Draw Times

Brazilian Mega-Sena draws are conducted twice a week on Wednesdays and Saturdays at approximately 8:00p.m. (Brasilia Standard Time - BRT). The draw time converts to Thursdays and Sundays, Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST).

Jackpot Rollover

There is no jackpot cap on Brazilian Mega-Sena, the jackpot continues to grow until it is won.

Tax

Brazilian income taxes are payable on Mega-Sena lottery winnings at a rate of 13.8%. Brazilian taxes are deducted from the prize amounts prior to the payout from the underlying lottery operator.

Promotional Prizes

We are unable to offer any local promotional prizes that are offered by the underlying lottery operators from time to time. The three different ways for players to win a prize as listed above are the valid prizes we offer.
