Canadian Lotto 6/49 Game Rules

General Overview

Game Format: Select 6 numbers from 1 to 49

Summary of Lottery Operator Rules

Selection of Numbers

To play Canadian Lotto 6/49, a player selects six numbers from a pool of 1 to 49. Players may select their own numbers or use the ‘quick pick’ function to have the numbers randomly selected.

Prizes and Odds

There are seven different ways for players to win a prize.

Prize Tier Match Estimated Prize Fund Odds
1 6 Numbers 79.5% * 1 in 13,986,816
2 5 Numbers + Bonus Ball 6% * 1 in 2,330,636
3 5 Numbers 5% * 1 in 55,492
4 4 Numbers 9.5% * 1 in 1,033
5 3 Numbers CA$10 1 in 56.7
6 2 Numbers + Bonus Ball CA$5 1 in 81.2
7 2 Numbers Free Ticket 1 in 8.3
The overall odds of winning a prize are 1 in 6.6.

*The percentage shown for prize tiers 1 through to 4, is the percentage of the ‘Estimated Prize Fund’ after the value of Prize Tiers 5, 6 and 7 have been deducted.

The underlying lottery is conducted on a pari-mutuel lottery basis, how much you win depends on the number of winning tickets in that same tier as well as the number of tickets sold in the particular underlying lottery draw. With the

exception of the ‘3 Numbers’, ‘2 Numbers + Bonus Ball’ and ‘2 Numbers’ prize categories (Prize Tiers 5, 6 and 7) which are fixed prizes.

Only one prize per game can be won in each draw, if a game qualifies as a winner across more than one prize category than the highest prize will be paid to the winning player.

The Guaranteed Prize Draw is a supplementary game where one ticket holder in the underlying lottery will win CA$1 million in each Canadian Lotto 6/49 draw. A player receives one ten-digit code for the Guaranteed Prize Draw for each game purchased in the particular draw. If the code matches the code drawn the player wins the CA$1 million prize.

Draw Times

Canadian Lotto 6/49 draws are conducted twice a week on Wednesdays and Saturdays at approximately 9:10p.m. (Canadian Eastern Standard Time). The draw time converts to Thursdays and Sundays, Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST).

Jackpot Rollover

There is no jackpot cap on Canadian Lotto 6/49, the jackpot continues to grow until it is won.

Jackpot Payout

Jackpot winners receive their prize as a lump-sum payment.

Tax

Lottery winnings are not currently taxed in Canada, where the matching tickets are purchased in the underlying lottery.

Promotional Prizes

We are unable to offer any local promotional prizes that are offered by the underlying lottery operators from time to time. The seven different ways for players to win a prize as listed above are the valid prizes we offer.
