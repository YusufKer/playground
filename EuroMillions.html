Summary of Lottery Operator Rules

Selection of Numbers

To play EuroMillions, a player selects five numbers from a pool of 1 to 50 and then two Lucky Star numbers from a pool of 1 to 12. Players may select their own numbers or use the ‘quick pick’ function to have the numbers randomly selected.

The overall odds of winning a prize are 1 in 13.

The percentage allocated to the jackpot is adjusted from 50% to 42% after five draws in a rollover series. The difference of 8% is allocated to a reserve fund to ensure the minimum jackpot can always be paid.

The remaining 1% of the prize fund is allocated to a reserve Booster Fund to ensure the minimum jackpot can always be paid. Occasionally a special jackpot draw is held using surplus funds from the Booster Funds.

The underlying lottery is conducted on a pari-mutuel lottery basis, how much you win depends on the number of winning tickets in that same tier as well as the number of tickets sold in the particular underlying lottery draw.

Only one prize per game can be won in each draw, if a game qualifies as a winner across more than one prize category than the highest prize will be paid to the winning player.

Draw Times

EuroMillions draws are conducted twice each week, on Tuesdays and Fridays with each draw taking place around 9:05p.m. (Central European Time) with results available shortly after. The draw time converts to Wednesdays and Saturdays, Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST).

Jackpot Rollover

There is a jackpot cap on EuroMillions, the minimum jackpot is €17 million and the jackpot continues to grow until it reaches the jackpot cap, currently set at €220 million. If the jackpot reaches this amount the jackpot can last for a maximum of five draws, if there is no winner in the fifth draw the jackpot is paid out to lower prize tiers. Each time the jackpot cap is reached and paid out, the jackpot cap increases by €10 million for the next cycle, this method can continue to a maximum of €250 million.

Jackpot Payout

Jackpot winners receive their prize as a lump-sum payment.

Tax

Lottery winnings are not currently taxed in Austria, where the matching tickets are purchased in the underlying lottery.

Promotional Prizes

We are unable to offer any local promotional prizes that are offered by the underlying lottery operators from time to time. The 13 different ways for players to win a prize as listed above are the valid prizes we offer.
